Meet Elizabeth

I grew up in the USA playing video games (Hello, World of Warcraft!), and later relocated to Vancouver, BC to earn an Environmental Science degree from the University of British Columbia. Life led me on a wild journey into landscape and garden design, culminating in the creation of my own company. I first encountered website design through creating a company website on SquareSpace, and genuinely enjoyed the process. While I loved being my own boss and growing a business, I yearned for more opportunities for growth and mental stimulation, which eventually led me to programming.

I’ve always enjoyed working with computers; Microsoft Excel was my favorite course in university! When Skillcrush popped up on a Google search with their free bootcamp course, I found myelf fascinated by HTML, CSS and website design. Building websites, creating something beautiful, personal, and impactful from nothing inspires me and the work I do. Making the switch into the tech industry was the easiest decision I've made in recent years, with the added bonus of pursuing my love of languages (looking at you, back end languages!).

Don't worry, I don't spend ALL of my time as a code monkey...
When I’m not at my desk listening to lo-fi beats and coding up a storm, you'll find me in a hammock reading a book, or exploring the mountains with my dogs.

My Process
Discover

I start by exploring the scope of the project (AKA I ask QUESTIONS!). Who is the audience? Is this a greenfield project or are we working with an existing product? What are the project goals? Which tools and resources can I utilize? These questions provide me with a framework to begin finding answers and resources.

Research

The next step is all about research. Finding answers to discovery inquiries, gathering resources, ideas, and inspiration for design and content. I examine a wide variety of information to tailor a unique solution for a website or product that will enhance desired features and goals.

Build

And lastly, the fun part -- building! I aboslutely love getting into the nitty gritty details for a project. Whether I am building from scratch, a mock up, or editing an existing project, the code is where the magic happens. It is the culmination of both the discovery and research phases, and by far my favorite part of the process.
Once I have a prototype made, the testing begins! I set up testing for each feature, and as I learn what works and what needs improvement, the project begins to come alive! This is also where feedback from the client, and troubleshooting comes into play.
